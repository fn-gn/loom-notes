\section*{The one idea, and how to read these notes}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{The one idea · how to read}
\warmth{0}

\begin{strand}
To minimize $f$, stand on its graph and walk downhill: $x_{k+1}=x_k-\eta\,\nabla f(x_k)$. How fast
you reach the bottom is set entirely by the \keyword{shape of the bowl}. The single idea of these
notes: the convergence rate is a \keyword{tax on conditioning}. A round bowl is paid off fast; a
long narrow valley slowly; and every acceleration trick, from momentum to preconditioning, is a
way to pay less of that tax.
\end{strand}

\block{The master table: the rate, and what sets it (fill it in as you go)}
\noindent\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l L@{}}
\toprule
{\color{indigo}assumptions on $f$} & {\color{madder}rate / steps to error $\varepsilon$}\\
\midrule
convex, $L$-smooth              & $f(x_k)-f^\star=O(1/k)$, so $O(\fillin[1.2cm])$ steps \;(§\ref{sec:smooth})\\
$\mu$-strongly convex, $L$-smooth & linear: $(1-1/\kappa)^k$, so $O(\kappa\log\tfrac1\varepsilon)$ \;(§\ref{sec:cond})\\
\quad condition number          & $\kappa=\fillin[1.5cm]$ (the elongation of the bowl)\\
accelerated (Nesterov)          & $O(\fillin[1.6cm]\log\tfrac1\varepsilon)$: the optimal first-order rate \;(§\ref{sec:faces})\\
stochastic (SGD)                & cheap steps, but a \fillin[2cm] floor\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
